## 2. WHAT IS UMWERO?

### Official Definition
The Umwero Alphabet is a Kinyarwanda writing system developed to authentically represent the language's unique phonemes and preserve its cultural essence. Unlike the Latin Kinyarwanda script, which often misrepresents native sounds, Umwero stays true to Kinyarwanda phonetic rules and the deep symbolism found in Rwandan traditions.

### Key Characteristics
- **39 Basic Phonemes**: Kinyarwanda has 39 major sounds, represented by 39 core characters
- **Ibihekane (Ligatures)**: Combined sounds that appear as single units, reflecting natural speech patterns
- **Cultural Symbolism**: Each character embodies elements of Rwandan culture, environment, and ancestral beliefs
- **Right-to-Left Writing**: The script is written from right to left
- **Measurement System**: Based on "8" (umunani) - symbolizing heritage and intellectual property passed to future generations

### The Name "Umwero"
Literally means "the harvest" but encompasses:
- **Imana** (God) - the holy one who blesses
- **In'ka** (Cattle) - white milk (umweru/ayera)
- **Ingoma** (Throne/Kingdom) - cultural sovereignty
- Represents cultivation, harvest, and cultural abundance

## 3. LINGUISTIC FOUNDATION

### The Problem Umwero Solves

#### Latin Script Inadequacies
1. **Phonetic Misrepresentation**:
   - "Rwanda" written but pronounced "RGWanda" (missing 'G' sound)
   - "TW" written but should be "TKW" (tukuwa not tua)
   - "RW" should be "RGW" to capture native pronunciation

2. **Colonial Influence**:
   - Latin alphabet imposed during colonization
   - Francophones wrote "C" while Anglophones wrote "CH" for same sound
   - Writing system created by colonizers who didn't understand Kinyarwanda phonetics

3. **Ongoing Confusion**:
   - Ministry of Culture changed writing rules in 2014, 2020, and planning changes in 2024
   - Proof that Latin alphabet cannot fully capture Kinyarwanda sounds

### Ibihekane vs. Ligatures

#### Ibihekane (Kinyarwanda Concept)
From "guhekana" - when a mother carries a baby on her back, appearing as one unit tied together. In Umwero:
- Multiple characters combine into single visual form
- Represents how sounds function naturally in Kinyarwanda
- Example: RGW = R + G + W (three sounds, one visual unit)
- W becomes smaller in ibihekane combinations (like tiny circle)

#### Not All Combinations Are Ibihekane
- SH, SHY, TS are single distinct phonemes, not combinations
- Each has standalone character in Umwero

#### Comparison Table (Latin to Umwero)

**5 Vowels**: A, E, I, O, U

**Basic Consonants**: B, D, F, G, H, J, K, M, N, P, R, S, T, V, W, Y, Z

**Ibihekane Examples**:
- NC, NCW, CW, DW, RY, NK, NKW
- MF, MFW, MFY, FW, FY, GW
- SH, NSH, NSHW, SHW, SHY, SHYW, NSHY, NSHYW
- PF, PFW, PFY, JW, KY, NKY, KW, JY, NJY
- MW, MY, MYW, ND, NDW, NDY, NW, NG, NGW
- NT, NTW, NNY, NYY, NZ, NZW
- MV, MVW, MVY, MPY, PW, PY, MP
- RW, SY, SW, NS, NSY, NSW, TW, TY
- NJ, NJW, VW, VY, TS, TSW, NY, NYW, ZW

**Special Character**: B (used only in ibihekane, sounds like English B)

## 4. CULTURAL ROOTS: THREE PILLARS

### Pillar 1: IMANA (God)

#### Theological Foundation
- **Hero na Herezo**: Alpha and Omega (beginning and ending)
- God has no beginning nor ending
- Symbolized by **Circle** (Uruziga) - no start, no end
- "Ibanze" - God as the starting of all things, including numbers

#### Cultural Practices
- Rituals performed inside circles to strengthen will, spells, prayers
- "Mu Urunana twunga Ubumwe" - "In the circle we unite"
- Family concept: husband, wife, child forming circle
- Traditional circular houses with prominent circle at entrance

#### In Umwero Script
- Circle represents life's cyclical nature
- Foundation of character design
- Unchangeable shape (unlike other letters that change in mirror)

### Pillar 2: IN'KA (Cattle)

#### Cultural Significance
- "In'ka ni Umunyarwanda" - "A cow is Rwandan"
- Symbol of wealth and prosperity
- "In'ka zifasha ababyeyi kurera abana" - "Cows help mothers raise children"
- Essential in social structure: dowry gift symbolizing union and prosperity

#### Inyambo Cows
- Long, graceful horns symbolizing beauty and elegance
- Featured in Amaraba dance (traditional dance for cows)
- Dancers raise arms to mimic Inyambo horns
- Vocalization: "Baaaa" sound

#### In Umwero Script
- Letter 'A': Inyambo cow's head and horns
- Letter 'B': Part of Inyambo body
- 'B' + 'A' together: Complete cow shape and sound "baaaaaa"

### Pillar 3: INGOMA (Throne/Kingdom)

#### Cultural Meaning
- Translated as "drum" but means "kingdom" or "throne"
- Royal drum symbolizes sovereignty
- King Gihanga Ngomijana: "Ngoma" (throne) + "Jana" (100) = King of 100 thrones
- "Gihanga" means creator

#### Personal Kingdom
- "Akami ka muntu ni umutima we" - "The kingdom of a person is in his heart"

#### In Umwero Script
- Throne symbol used in numbers for hundreds (honoring Gihanga's 100 thrones)
- Throne above number 2 = 200
- Throne on first letter indicates proper name
- Not yet functional in digital font (planned feature)

## 5. CHARACTER-BY-CHARACTER CULTURAL SIGNIFICANCE

### Letter A (Ah) - Inyambo Cow's Head
- **Visual**: Head and horns of Inyambo cow
- **Cultural Connection**: Wealth, sustenance, nourishment
- **Proverb**: "Inka ni Umunyarwanda, zifasha kurera abana ba Nyina"
- **Function**: Vowel
- **Similar to**: Egyptian hieroglyph 'A' from ox (but independently derived from Rwandan culture)

### Letter R (Ra) - God/Praising
- **Visual**: Person kneeling in reverence (rotated for readability)
- **Cultural Connection**: God as "Ra" or "Rurema" (Creator)
- **Linguistic**: "Ra" + "Urema" = "Rurema" (following Kinyarwanda syntax)
- **Symbolism**: Worship and devotion to divine
- **Note**: NOT derived from Egyptian Ra, coincidental similarity

### Letter B (Ba) - Inyambo Cow's Body
- **Visual**: Part of Inyambo cow's body
- **Cultural Connection**: Combined with 'A' forms complete cow
- **Sound**: "Baaaa" vocalization of Inyambo
- **Function**: Consonant representing cultural pride and wealth

### Letter M (Ma) - Womb/Motherhood
- **Visual**: Baby within womb connected to umbilical cord
- **Cultural Connection**: Maternity, creation, continuity
- **Linguistic**: "Mama," "Maawe," "Maaye" - maternal terms
- **Proverb**: "Iyo abyaye, twunguka amaboko" - "When she gives birth, we gain more hands/power"
- **Derivatives**: L, LL, G, Z (directly derived through Umura/umbilical cord concept)
- **Indirect Relations**: Letters sharing morphological similarities with ancestry/kinship

#### Umura (Umbilical Cord) Concept
- **Proverb**: "Umurajye Nyina yamuraze, nawe nabyara umwana azawumuraga"
- **Translation**: "The heritage one inherits from their mother will be passed to their own child"
- **In Script**: Umura + letter creates direct lineage connections

### Letter T (Ta) & K (Ka) - Featural Letters
- **Phonetic Basis**: Based on articulatory features
- **IPA Alignment**:
  - K = [k] (IPA 109) - voiceless velar plosive
  - T = [t] (IPA 103) - voiceless alveolar plosive
- **Visual**: When flipped (right-to-left script), align with IPA symbols
- **Similar to**: Hangul's featural design
- **Shared Feature**: Both have same horizontal line showing similarity

### Letter S (Sa) & TS (Tsa) - Sibilant Sounds
- **Characteristic**: Unstoppable, continuous airflow
- **Visual**: Flowing, uninterrupted strokes
- **Phonetic**: Can be extended indefinitely ("Siiiiiiiii")
- **Design**: Similar structure, different positioning
- **Symbolism**: Never-ending, constant motion

### Letter C (Ca/Cha) - Serpent/Pathway
- **Visual**: Resembles serpent (inzoka y'incira) and pathway
- **Cultural Expressions**:
  - "Guca akenge" - To gain wisdom
  - "Guca mu nzira" - To pass through a path
  - "Ca aha" - Pass here
- **Symbolism**: Wisdom, movement, transition, enlightenment
- **Connection**: Serpent traditionally associated with wisdom in Kinyarwanda

### Letter J (Ja) - Slave/Laborer
- **Visual**: Bowed figure showing submission
- **Linguistic**: "Umu" + "Ja" = "Umuja" (slave/laborer)
- **Proverb**: "Umuja ni umugonda Josi" - "A slave bows"
- **Cultural Context**: Represents servitude, humility, respect, deference
- **Dual Meaning**: Both submission and respectful reverence

### Letter D (Da) - Masculinity/Father
- **Visual**: Resembles male gender symbol (♂), oriented upward
- **Contrast**: M (feminine) oriented downward
- **Linguistic**: "Data" (Father), "Daye" (My father), "Dawe" (Our father)
- **Function**: Also used for addressing/pointing out (like "you")
- **Symbolism**: Fatherhood, identity, recognition

### Letter MF (Mfa) - Noble Person/Imfura
- **Visual**: Oblique line (person in distress) + two half-circles
  - Upper-back circle: "gufata mu mugongo" (supporting in trouble)
  - Lower-front circle: "gufata mu nda" (providing sustenance)
- **Cultural Concept**: Imfura (firstborn, noble, respected person)
- **Proverb**: "Iyo Imfura igufashe mu mugongo, ntisiga no mu nda"
- **Translation**: "When an Imfura supports you, they lift your burden and ensure sustenance"
- **Significance**: Embodies moral duty, generosity, leadership
- **Unique**: Represents philosophical concept, not physical object

### Letter PF (Pfa) - Death/Destruction
- **Visual**: Circle (life) intersected by horizontal line (disruption)
- **Linguistic**: "Gupfa" (to die), "Yapfuye" (s/he died)
- **Symbolism**: Circle = Uruziga (life's cycle), Line = destruction of life
- **Concept**: Hero na Herezo (Alpha and Omega) disrupted
- **Cultural**: Transition from life to death, termination of life cycle

## 6. DEVELOPMENT PROCESS

### Origin Story

#### Personal Journey (2014-2019)
1. **2014**: Kwizera Mugisha begins writing Kinyarwanda rap songs
2. **Discovery**: Notices discrepancy between spoken and written Kinyarwanda
3. **2017**: Visits Nairobi, Kenya - observes different writing systems for similar Bantu sounds
4. **Realization**: Latin alphabet imposed during colonization doesn't accommodate Bantu phonetics
5. **2019**: First year at university (Computer Science) - breakthrough moment

#### The Mirror Revelation (2019)
- Looking in mirror: Circle never changes, unlike other letters
- Human eyes: Cross sign naturally (right eye sees left eye)
- Realization: "Alphabet is like human body - can die or come to life"
- Character = body of spirit (sound)
- Started with unchangeable circle shape

### Design Principles

#### The Measurement of 8 (Umunani)
- All characters uniform height using measure of 8
- **Cultural Significance**: Number 8 symbolizes heritage/inheritance
- "Umunani" = share of estate given by parents to children
- Represents intellectual property heritage for future generations
- **Structure**: 8 = 1+2+3+2 (specific proportional system)

#### Design Approach
- **80% Cultural**: Based on Kinyarwanda culture and traditions
- **20% Intuitive**: Some letters created intuitively, cultural connections discovered later
- **Examples**:
  - Cultural reference: A, B, M, D, Throne symbol
  - Intuitive then cultural: J, MF, Y
- **Creator's Belief**: "Not just me who created Umwero but Imana (God)"

### Collaborative Development
- **Craig Cornelius**: Helped develop virtual Umwero keyboard
- **18 Students**: Learned alphabet in remarkably short period, provided feedback
- **Refinement**: Made script intuitive and user-friendly
- **Digital Tools**: Virtual keyboard created (throne feature not yet functional)

## 7. PHONETIC & LINGUISTIC STRUCTURE

### IPA (International Phonetic Alphabet) Alignment

The Umwero script aligns with IPA standards for phonetic accuracy:

#### Vowels
- A = [a] (IPA 304)
- E = [e] (IPA 302)
- I = [i] (IPA 301)
- O = [o] (IPA 307)
- U = [u] (IPA 308)

#### Key Consonants
- B = [b] (IPA 102)
- D = [d] (IPA 104)
- F = [f] (IPA 128)
- G = [g]
- H = [h]
- J = [dʒ]
- K = [k] (IPA 109)
- M = [m] (IPA 114)
- N = [n] (IPA 116)
- P = [p] (IPA 101)
- R = [r]
- S = [s]
- T = [t] (IPA 103)
- V = [v]
- W = [w]
- Y = [j] (IPA 153)
- Z = [z]

#### Special Sounds
- SH = [ʃ] (IPA 146/147)
- TS = [ts]
- C/CH = [tʃ] (IPA 135/137)
- NG = [ŋ] (IPA 119)

### Nasal Sounds
- MM = nasal sound of M
- NN = nasal sound of N

### Bantu Language Variations
- Some characters used in other Bantu languages (Kirundi, Kiswahili, Lingala, Kinyamulenge)
- Example: DZA used in Kirundi
- Umwero designed for broader Bantu language family while rooted in Kinyarwanda

## 8. NUMERICAL SYSTEM

### Basic Numbers
- 1-9: Individual characters
- 0: Circle (representing Imana/God as Ibanze - starting of numbers)

### Place Value System
- **Times 10**: Specific symbol
- **Times 100**: Throne symbol (honoring Gihanga Ngomijana)
- **Times 1000**: Specific symbol
- **Times 10^4 through 10^9**: Progressive symbols

### Cultural Integration
- God as "Ibanze" (starting) of numbers
- "Kubanza" = "Kuba" (to be) + "Nza" (will) = "I will be"
- All things from God are god but not the Creator
- Circle represents Hero na Herezo in numbers

## 9. CULTURAL ARTIFACTS & TRADITIONS

### Amasunzu (Traditional Hairstyle)
- **Meaning**: "Umurima w'umuntu ni umutwe we, ubwe"
- **Translation**: "The person's field of innovation/success is their mind"
- **Philosophy**: Think before taking action
- **Connection**: Intellectual heritage embedded in Umwero

### Amaraba (Traditional Dance)
- **Two Types**: War dance and cattle dance
- **Cattle Dance**: Dancers raise arms mimicking Inyambo horns
- **Connection**: Visual representation in Umwero letters A and B

### Imigongo (Decorative Art)
- Traditional Rwandan decorative art
- Part of intellectual property heritage
- Reflects cultural aesthetics

### Urugori (Parental Throne)
- Adornment worn by mothers after giving birth
- Symbolizes elevated status and sacred role
- "Crown of motherhood"
- Represents participation in divine creation process

### Traditional Architecture
- Circular houses reflecting cultural values
- Prominent circle at entrance symbolizing family unity
- Circle = unbroken chain of family continuity
